# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud has become one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India due to the widespread use of digital payment apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm. This type of fraud mainly targets individuals who are not fully aware of how digital transactions work.

A common scenario begins when a victim posts an item for sale online or is expecting a payment. The fraudster contacts the victim pretending to be a genuine buyer or bank representative. They then send a fake payment request or QR code, claiming that it is required to "receive" money. In some cases, they create urgency by saying the offer will expire soon or that immediate action is required.

The victim, believing the request to be legitimate, clicks on the link or scans the QR code and enters their UPI PIN. However, instead of receiving money, the action authorizes a payment from the victim’s account. Within seconds, money is transferred to the fraudster’s account.

Such scams primarily target students, small sellers, and elderly individuals who may not fully understand the difference between sending and receiving money through UPI. The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and reduced trust in digital payment systems.

With increasing digital adoption in India, such fraud cases are rising rapidly. This highlights the importance of awareness, careful verification, and responsible usage of online payment platforms to prevent falling victim to such scams.
